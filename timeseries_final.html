windowArimaOrder<-function(stk){
  end=floor(length(stk)/100)
  window=100
  upper=window+1
  lower=1
  windows=NULL
  mods=vector("list",end)
  for (i in 1:end) {
    stk_mod=auto.arima(stk[lower:upper])
    mods[[i]]=stk_mod
    windows=c(windows,paste0(lower,"-",upper))
    upper=upper+window
    lower=lower+window
  }
  orders=sapply(mods,arimaorder)
  od=apply(orders,MARGIN=2,function(z) gsub(", ","",toString(z)))
  freq=table(od)
  od=rbind(windows,od)
  return(list(freq,od,mods))
}

#eval=F
windowsuccess<-function(stk){
  stk=stk[(length(stk)-200):length(stk)]
  window=100
  upper=window
  lower=1
  windows=NULL
  end=upper

  data=data.frame(holts_success=character(),arima_success=character())
  for (i in 1:end) {
    actual=stk[(upper+1)]
    prev=stk[upper]
  
    stk_mod_arima=auto.arima(stk[lower:upper])
    arima=forecast(stk_mod_arima,h=1)$mean[[1]]
  
    stk_mod_holt=HoltWinters(stk[lower:upper],gamma=F,beta=F)
    holts=forecast(stk_mod_holt,h=1)$mean[[1]]
  
    windows=paste0(lower,"-",upper)
    
    tmp_holt = ifelse(((actual >= prev) & (holts >= prev))|((actual <= prev) & (holts <= prev)),
                          "success", "failure")
    
    tmp_arima= ifelse(((actual >= prev) & (arima >= prev))|((actual <= prev) & (arima <= prev)),
                          "success", "failure")
    tmp=data.frame(holts_success=tmp_holt,arima_success=tmp_arima)
  
    data=rbind(data,tmp)

    upper=upper+1
    lower=lower+1
  }
  h=as.vector(table(data$holts_success))
  a=as.vector(table(data$arima_success))
  return(list(holts_suc=h[1],holts_fail=h[2],arima_suc=a[1],arima_fail=a[2]))
}

Problem 2) Consider the classical random walk with absorbing barriers at 0 and N (gamblers ruin) where a gambler starts with k dollars, bets 1 dollar at each step, wins 1 dollar with probability p and loses his 1 dollar stake with probability q=1-p; he either wins (N-k) dollars to get his fortune to N dollars (the random walk gets absorbed at barrier N) and buys a car or goes bankrupt and quits (the random walk gets absorbed at barrier 0). Find the average number of the random walk steps until hitting the absorbing barriers.

Let \(D_{k}\) be the average number of steps until getting absorbed by barriers starting with \(k\) dollars. Using,
\[ E(X)=E_{Y}(E_{X}(X|Y)) \] \(\Longrightarrow\)

\[ \begin{equation} \begin{split} D_{k} &= p(1+D_{k+1})+(1-p)(1+D_{k-1}) \\ &= pD_{k+1}+1+(1-p)D_{k-1} \\ &\Longrightarrow pD_{k+1}-D_{k}+(1-p)D_{k-1}=-1 \\ \end{split} \end{equation} \] We have a nonhomogenious difference equation, finding homogenious solution with characteristic polynomial next,

\[ pD_{k+1}-D_{k}+(1-p)D_{k-1}=0 \] \(\Longrightarrow\)

\[ px^2-x+(1-p)=0 \]

The characteristic polynomial has roots,

\[ \begin{equation} \begin{split} x_{1} &= 1 \\ x_{2} &=\frac{(1-p)}{p} \end{split} \end{equation} \] \(\Longrightarrow\) the solution is of the form,

\[ \begin{equation} \begin{split} D_{k,H} &= C_{1}x_{1}^k + C_{2}x_{2}^k \\ &= C_{1} + C_{2}\bigg(\frac{(1-p)}{p}\bigg)^k \end{split} \end{equation} \] We need the particular solution to fully solve the difference equation, guess \(D_{k,P}=ak+b\). plug into the initial difference equation to get,

\[ \begin{equation} \begin{split} -1&=pD_{k+1}-D_{k}+(1-p)D_{k-1} \\ &= p(a(k+1)+b)-(ak+b)+(1-p)(a(k-1)+b) \\ &= pak+pa+bp-ak-b+ak-a+b-pak+pa-pb \\ &= 2pa-a \\ &= a(2p-1) \\ \Longrightarrow a = \frac{1}{1-2p} \end{split} \end{equation} \]

choice of \(b\) can be any arbitrary real number, hence we can set \(b=0\).

\[ \begin{equation} \begin{split} D_{k} &= D_{k,H}+D_{k,P}\\ &= C_{1} + C_{2}\bigg(\frac{(1-p)}{p}\bigg)^k + \frac{k}{1-2p} \end{split} \end{equation} \]

For initial conditions, note that if \(k=0\), then there are no expected number of steps to be at barrier 0, similarly with barrier N. \[ D_{0}=0 , \quad D_{N}=0\]

Using this to solve \(C_{1},C_{2}\), \[ D_{0}=C_{1}+C_{2}=0 \quad \Longrightarrow C_{1}=-C_{2} \] and,

\[ D_{N}=C_{1}-C_{1}\bigg(\frac{(1-p)}{p}\bigg)^N + \frac{N}{1-2p} = 0 \\ C_{1}\Big( 1-\bigg(\frac{(1-p)}{p}\bigg)^N \Big) = \frac{N}{2p-1} \\ \Longrightarrow C_{1}= \frac{\frac{N}{2p-1}}{ 1-\bigg(\frac{(1-p)}{p}\bigg)^N} \]

Then, the final solution is,

\[ D_{k}= \frac{\frac{N}{2p-1}}{ 1-\bigg(\frac{(1-p)}{p}\bigg)^N} -\frac{\frac{N}{2p-1}}{1-\bigg(\frac{(1-p)}{p}\bigg)^N}\bigg(\frac{(1-p)}{p}\bigg)^k+\frac{k}{1-2p} \]
and after simplifying a little,

\[ D_{k}= \frac{N\Big( 1-\bigg(\frac{(1-p)}{p}\bigg)^k \Big)-k}{ \Big( 1-\bigg(\frac{(1-p)}{p}\bigg)^N \Big) (2p-1)} \]

Problem 4) Exercise 10.2 on page 246.

an AR model has AR characteristic polynomial

\[ (1-1.6x+0.7x^2)(1-0.8x^{12}) \]

a) is the model stationary?

the process is of the form,

\[ (1-\phi_{1}x-\phi_{2}x^2)(1-\Phi_1 x^{12}) \]

with \(\phi_{1}=1.6\), \(\phi_{2}=-0.7\), and \(\phi=0.8\)

for an AR(2) process, we require that,

\[ \phi_{1}+\phi_{2}<1 , \quad \phi_{2}-\phi_{1}<1 , \quad |\phi_{2}|<1 , \quad |\Phi_{1}|<1 \] we check that,

\[ \phi_{1}+\phi_{2}= 0.9 < 1 \\ \phi_{2}-\phi_{1}=-2.3 < 1 \\ |\phi_{2}|=0.7<1 \\ |\Phi_{1}|=0.8<1 \]

\(\Longrightarrow\) the model is stationary

b) identify the model

the model is \(ARIMA(2,0,0)\times(1,0,0)_{12}\), since there is no MA, seasonal MA, differencing, or seasonal differencing.
